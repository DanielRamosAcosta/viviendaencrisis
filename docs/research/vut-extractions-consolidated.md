### C. DESPLAZAMIENTO Y GENTRIFICACIÓN

#### Theoretical Framework
- **Jansen-Verbeke (1998)**: coined "touristification" as irreversible urban change
- **Cócola-Gant (2018)**: taxonomy of 4 displacement types (direct, by exclusion, by deterioration, collective)
- **Hernández Cordero (2021)**: turistificación = substitution of stable population by floating population

#### Barcelona
- García-López et al. (2020): 2.06% of all dwellings listed on Airbnb (2015); 6.84% of rental dwellings
- 46% of legal tourist apartments concentrated in Eixample (ElDiario.es)
- 56% of licensees are legal entities; 8.8% individuals control 51% (PAH/SAGE)
- >70% of commercial premises in historic center linked to tourism (Amics de la Rambla 2023)
- First anti-tourism protest: August 2014, Barceloneta
- ABDT (Assemblea de Barris pel Decreixement Turístic) founded 2015
- Cócola-Gant (2011): Barri Gòtic → "de símbolo nacional a parque temático"
- Agència de Salut Pública: "sentimientos de tristeza, rabia, soledad, estrés e incluso alienación"

#### Madrid (Lavapiés)
- Mazorra Rodríguez (2023): VUT >12% of total housing; ~25% of rental in Distrito Centro
- Lost 10,000 residents in 5 years (>5/day)
- >300 traditional shops closed in Malasaña (2008-2022)
- Fondos (Grupo Elix) buying entire buildings for VUT conversion
- FRAVM: 450+ complaints processed

#### Palma de Mallorca
- Vives-Miró & Rullan (2017): "desposesión de vivienda por turistización" in Centro Histórico
- Correlation between evictions, property revaluation, and touristification
- +50% noise complaints in high-VUT zones (Policía Local)
- Manifestation July 21, 2024: ~50,000 people

#### Canarias
- Rent increase +68% (highest nationally, Pisos.com)
- ~1/3 reduction in housing available for residential use
- 33.8% population at risk of social exclusion
- April 20, 2024: 57,000 (police) to 200,000+ (organizers) — largest mobilization in Canary Islands history
- Protests replicated October 2024, May 2025; inspired Barcelona, Palma, Málaga, Sevilla, San Sebastián
- 55.3% of VUT managed by foreign entities/owners (Ley 6/2025)

#### Commercial Fabric
- ~50,000 neighborhood shops closed 2019-2024 (UATAE), avg 25/day
- Barrero Rescalvo & Jover Báez (2021): documented in Seville (Calle San Luis)
